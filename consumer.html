INSIGHTS Key Consumer Patterns
💳
Credit Card Stress Accelerating
Card 90+ DPD at 2.94% — highest since 2010 recovery. Subprime segment at 11.3%. Bottom-two income quintiles exhausted pandemic savings by mid-2024. Revolving debt hit $1.3T.
🏠
Housing Affordability at Historic Worst
Median household needs 43% of gross income for median home at 6.11% rates. Lock-in effect: 65% of mortgages below 4%. Existing sales at 30-year low. New builds filling gap with rate buy-downs.
💰
Real Wages Finally Positive
Nominal AHE +3.3% YoY with CPI at 2.4% = real wage growth of +0.9% annualized. First sustained positive real wages since 2020. Supports consumption but below +1.5% of the 2010s expansion.
📊
Consumer Bifurcation Deepening
Top 40% (asset owners, equity beneficiaries) spending robustly. Bottom 40% cutting discretionary. Luxury and value retail outperform; mid-market squeezed. Two-speed consumer economy.
⛽
Oil Shock Threatens Budgets
WTI at $94.65 (Mar 2026 Hormuz crisis). A $10/bbl rise adds ~$0.25/gal at pumps. Bottom quintile spends 8-10% of income on gas vs 2-3% for top quintile — regressive inflation pressure.
📉
Savings Buffer Critically Thin
Personal saving rate 4.5% vs 7-8% pre-pandemic norm. Excess pandemic savings ($2.1T peak) fully depleted. Any employment shock hits consumers with minimal runway.
